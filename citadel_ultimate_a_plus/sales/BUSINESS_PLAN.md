# Business Plan - Citadel Outbound Ops

## 1) Business Overview

Citadel is a local-service outbound lead generation business. It runs a repeatable pipeline for service operators:
- qualify target accounts,
- generate personalized outbound messaging,
- track funnel progression from first touch to reply and closed business.

Primary initial focus: local plumbing and HVAC operators in Harris County, TX.

## 2) Problem and Solution

### Problem

Local service operators often depend on expensive shared lead channels and inconsistent manual outreach. Common issues:
- low lead quality,
- weak follow-through,
- poor visibility into funnel performance.

### Solution

Citadel provides a structured outbound operating layer:
- campaign setup and targeting,
- consistent outreach execution,
- dashboard-level reporting for reply and conversion outcomes.

## 3) Target Market and Size

Core launch market:
- Plumbing + HVAC operators in Harris County, TX.

Market size assumption (provided):
- ~4,000+ plumbing/HVAC operators in Harris County alone.

Practical initial target:
- Focus on owner-operated and small multi-crew shops that need booked jobs without adding internal outbound headcount.

## 4) Revenue Model

Pricing model:
- Setup fee: **$1,500** (one-time per client)
- Monthly recurring fee: **$1,500 per client**

Revenue mechanics:
- Month 1 with new client: $3,000 total billings ($1,500 setup + $1,500 monthly)
- Ongoing months: $1,500 recurring per active client

## 5) Financial Projections and Break-Even

### Key assumptions (lean operating model)

- Monthly fixed operating costs: **$3,000**
- Variable delivery cost per client/month: **$350**
- Contribution per client/month: **$1,150** ($1,500 - $350)

### Break-even math

- Break-even client count = Fixed Costs / Contribution per Client
- Break-even = $3,000 / $1,150 = **2.61 clients**
- Operational break-even target = **3 active clients**

### 3-client scenario

- Recurring revenue: **$4,500/month**
- Variable costs: **$1,050/month**
- Contribution after variable costs: **$3,450/month**
- Less fixed costs ($3,000): **$450/month operating surplus**

Cash effect of onboarding first 3 clients:
- Setup revenue: **$4,500 total** (3 x $1,500) during onboarding period

### 12-month base-case projection (planning scenario)

Assumption:
- Add 1 net new client per month for first 6 months,
- then maintain and optimize retention.

Illustrative run-rate:
- End of Month 3: 3 clients -> $4,500 MRR
- End of Month 6: 6 clients -> $9,000 MRR
- End of Month 12: 9 clients -> $13,500 MRR

## 6) Use of Funds (First 90 Days)

Total recommended 90-day operating budget: **$12,000**

Allocation:
- Sending infrastructure (inbox tooling, deliverability, warmup, sending software): **$4,800**
- Domain and inbox costs (domains, DNS, mailbox provisioning/rotation): **$1,800**
- Working capital (contractor support, ops buffer, contingencies): **$5,400**

Use-of-funds objective:
- Reach and stabilize first 3 active clients,
- maintain campaign deliverability and continuity,
- preserve enough buffer to sustain operations through onboarding cycle.

## 7) Go-to-Market Plan (Initial)

- Primary channel: direct outbound to plumbing/HVAC operators.
- Sales motion: short discovery -> pilot offer -> 30-day execution with weekly KPI review.
- Offer framing: lower dependency on shared lead platforms and improve outbound consistency.

## 8) Milestones

90-day milestones:
- 3 active paying clients
- $4,500 MRR
- at least one documented case study with lead/reply/conversion metrics

180-day milestones:
- 6 active clients
- $9,000 MRR
- repeatable onboarding and delivery cadence

## 9) Key Risks and Mitigations

- Deliverability risk -> enforce inbox hygiene and sending controls.
- Client retention risk -> weekly KPI reporting and rapid optimization loops.
- Capacity risk -> standardize onboarding templates and delivery SOPs.

This plan is designed as an operating document and should be updated monthly with actual conversion, churn, and margin data.
