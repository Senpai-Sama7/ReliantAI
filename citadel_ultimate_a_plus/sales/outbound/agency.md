- 5 min: how many local service clients, what verticals, current outbound workflow

- 5 min: walkthrough of per-client pipeline and dashboard

## Discovery Questions

- How many local service clients are you managing outbound for today?
- What does your current outbound stack look like per client?
- How many hours/week does outbound take per client?
- What's your biggest constraint on taking more clients — capacity or results?
- Who owns outbound execution day-to-day — in-house team or VAs?
- What does your client reporting look like today?

## Objection Responses

`"We already have a system."`
- Makes sense. Most agencies I talk to have something — the question is whether it scales without adding headcount. Happy to compare approaches if useful.

`"We don't want to change tools mid-engagement."`
- Understood. Most agencies pilot with one new client first, then migrate existing clients if the economics work. No disruption to current engagements.

`"Our clients are too small for this."`
- The per-client cost is $500-$1,000/month. If a client's average job value is $500+, one closed deal covers the monthly cost. The question is whether the volume is there.

`"Not now."`
- No pressure. If helpful, I can send the agency economics one-pager so you have it when capacity becomes the bottleneck.
