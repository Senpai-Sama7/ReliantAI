# Call Script — Discovery Call (15-20 min)

Works for both operator and agency ICPs. Adapt the questions based on who you're talking to.

## Before the Call

- Review their website (you should already have a `{{specific_observation}}`)
- Check their Google Business Profile (rating, review count, recent reviews)
- Know their vertical and geography
- Have the dashboard open with a sample pipeline for their vertical

## Opening (2 min)

> "Hey {{first_name}}, thanks for making time. I'll keep this tight — 15 minutes, and if it's not relevant we'll wrap early. Sound good?"

> "Quick context: we build outbound pipelines for [plumbing operators / agencies managing local service clients]. I reached out because I noticed [specific observation]. Before I show you anything, I want to understand what you're doing today so I can tell you if this is actually a fit."

## Discovery — Operator ICP (5-7 min)

Ask in order. Stop and dig in when you hear pain.

1. "Where are most of your jobs coming from right now — referrals, Angi, Google, something else?"
2. "Roughly what are you paying per lead on those channels?"
3. "Out of 10 leads that come in, how many actually turn into booked jobs?"
4. "Have you tried any kind of outbound — cold email, mailers, door-knocking?"
5. "What happened?" (if yes to #4)
6. "If I could show you a way to get qualified leads at half your current cost-per-lead, with full visibility into what's working — is that worth 15 more minutes?"

## Discovery — Agency ICP (5-7 min)

1. "How many local service clients are you running outbound for right now?"
2. "Walk me through what that looks like per client — tools, process, who does the work?"
3. "How many hours per week does outbound take per client?"
4. "What's the biggest bottleneck — finding enough prospects, writing personalized copy, or keeping execution consistent?"
5. "When you add a new client, how long does it take to get outbound running for them?"
6. "If you could cut per-client outbound time by 80% and deploy the same pipeline across every client in a vertical — is that worth seeing?"

## Demo (5 min)

Only show this if discovery confirmed a real pain. If not, skip to wrap.

> "Let me show you what this looks like in practice. I'm going to use [their vertical] in [their geography]."

Walk through:
1. Run a sample prospect through the pipeline (30 seconds — show the scout + qualify output)
2. Show the generated outreach draft (point out the 7-beat structure, but don't call it that — just say "structured messaging that actually gets replies")
3. Show the dashboard — funnel view, lead timeline, reply tracking
4. Show the economics view — cost per lead, cost per reply, cost per meeting

> "This is what your pipeline looks like after week 1. Every lead tracked from first touch to close."

## Qualification Check (2 min)

After the demo, ask:

> "Based on what you've seen — does this solve a real problem for you, or is this a nice-to-have?"

If real problem → move to pilot proposal.
If nice-to-have → thank them and follow up in 30 days.

## Close / Next Step (2 min)

> "Here's what I'd suggest: we run a 30-day pilot. I configure your vertical and geography, load your first prospect batch, and you see results within the first week. Setup is $1,500, pilot month is $1,500. No long-term contract — the results speak for themselves."

> "I can send the pilot scope doc today. If it looks right, we can start configuration this week and have your first outreach going by [specific date — 3-4 business days out]."

If they need to think about it:
> "Totally fair. I'll send the scope doc and a sample report. Can we reconnect [specific day] to make a call?"

Always leave with a specific next step and date. Never end with "let me know."

Quick version: we run outbound for [plumbing operators / agencies] in [city] — qualified leads, tracked pipeline, no shared leads. If that's relevant, my number is [number]. Either way, I'll follow up by email."
